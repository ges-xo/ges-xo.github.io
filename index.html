All Things Hamilton

Lin - Manuel Miranda

Read time: 25 minutes

Lin-Manuel Miranda was born January 16, 1980 in New York. He is an American actor, composer lyricist, and writer who created and starred in stage productions that blend modern musical forms, such as hip-hop, with classical musical theatre.

He was born to parents of Puerto Rican origin and grew up in a Hispanic neighbourhood in northern Manhattan. His father was a political consultant to several New York City mayors, and his mother was a psychologist.

In high school he won the lead tole in W.S. Gilbert and Arthur Sullivan's Pirates of Penzance and became a fixture in the school's drama program. He studied theatre at Wesleyan University, where he continued to perform in musicals and to write his own songs and shows. It was there that he created the first draft of the musical In the Heights, which was set in Washington Heights, a northern Manhattan neighbourhood similar to that of his childhood.

After reading Ron Chernow's 2004 biography about Alexander Hamilton, he began creating a musical about the Founding Father. The resulting Hamilton was energetic and infectious, and it features a racially diverse set, with Lin-Manuel Miranda starring the title role.

Later on, he contributed to the soundtrack for the Disney animated film Moana (2016), and he earned an Academy Award nomination - his first - for the song "How Far I'll Go". Also, in 2021, he wrote "Dos Orguitas" for the film Encanto, and the song earned him another Oscar nomination.

Background of Alexander Hamilton

Alexander Hamilton was a transformative figure in American history, known for his intelligence, vision, and relentless drive. Born out of wedlock on the island of Nevis in the Caribbean in 1755, Hamilton’s early life was shaped by hardship, including the death of his mother and his father’s abandonment. Despite these challenges, his exceptional intellect and writing abilities caught the attention of local patrons, who helped send him to the American colonies for education. He enrolled at King’s College (now Columbia University) in New York, where he became actively involved in revolutionary politics, advocating for American independence from Britain.

During the American Revolution, Hamilton gained Washington’s trust and became a key advisor. His experience in the war deepened his belief in the need for a strong federal government, which he later championed as a delegate to the Constitutional Convention in 1787. Hamilton’s contributions to the Federalist Papers, alongside James Madison and John Jay, were instrumental in persuading states to ratify the U.S. Constitution. In these essays, Hamilton argued for a centralised government that could manage its debts and resources. As the first Secretary of the Treasury under President Washington, Hamilton’s financial plan reshaped the nation’s economic landscape. His policies laid the groundwork for the United States' modern economic system and created a foundation for its future growth.

Hamilton’s personal life was equally dramatic. His affair with Maria Reynolds became one of the first major political sex scandals in American history, and his political enemies used it to tarnish his reputation. His rivalry with Aaron Burr, another ambitious politician, grew increasingly hostile. This animosity led to a fatal duel in 1804, where Burr shot Hamilton in Weehawken, New Jersey, leading to his death the next day.

Despite his death, Hamilton's influence on American politics, economics, and governance has endured. His writings, particularly on federalism and economic policy, remain foundational texts for understanding the U.S. political system. Today, his legacy is celebrated as a visionary who helped shape the nation's identity, balancing revolutionary ideals with practical governance. His life and work continue to resonate, thanks to his monumental contributions to the founding of the United States.

Hamilton the Musical

Hamilton's music and lyrics weere written by Lin - Manuel Miranda. The original play, starring the original cast, is directed by Thomas Kail, with choreography by Andy Blankenbuehler and musical supervision and orchestrations by Alex Lacamoire. It is based on Ron Chernow's biography of Alexander Hamilton. The explosively popular, critically acclaimed musical combines hip-hop and Broadway in a way that has never been seen before. Lin-Manuel Miranda's work lifted Hamilton higher in the pantheon of the Founding Fathers, while humanising him in touching and inspiring ways. Many people consider this musical to be the most significant new American musical in this generation.

The story of Alexander Hamilton speaks to audiences on so many levels. The motifs of revolution and fighting for one's rights, the power of people coming together to fight for a common cause, a celebration of freedom and democracy, and its messages of hope and perseverance resonate deeply with audiences. The resounding success of Hamilton is undeniable. Not only has it become one of the most popular musicals of our time, but the show is also building future generations of theatre lovers, that are drawn to the music. The musical is a testament to the power of art and the importance of representation in theatre. Hamilton's fantastic score and deeper themes have made the story of one of America's Founding Fathers relevant worldwide.

The set design by David Korins is equally noteworthy, employing a minimalist and versatile approach that allows for fluid scene transitions and focuses attention on the performers. The use of a turntable stage adds a dynamic element, symbolizing the constant movement and change in the political landscape. The period-appropriate yet stylised costumes and lighting design, further immerse the audience in the late 18th and early 19th centuries while maintaining a contemporary edge.

Performance

The Musical is a dynamic showcase of talent, combining powerful acting, singing, and dancing to bring history to life in an innovative way. The originl cast, led by Lin-Manuel Miranda as Alexander Hamilton, delivers emotionally charged performances that convey the complex personalities and motivations of the characters. Each actor brings a unique energy to their role, with standout performances like Leslie Odom Jr.'s nuanced portrayal of Aaron Burr, capturing both his ambition and inner conflict. The ensemble cast plays a critical role as well, transitioning seamlessly between characters and scenes, while maintaining the high energy of the show. Their commitment to the storytelling, through both subtle and bold moments, ensures that the themes of ambition, legacy, and revolution are noticed constantly throughout the play.

Hamilton, premiered Off-Broadway on February 17, 2015, at the Public Theatre in New York City before moving to Broadway for a second opening on August 6. The musical has been staged at the Victoria Palace Theatre in London since December 2017.

In 2016 Hamilton was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for Drama, and it received an unprecedented 16 Tony nominations. Lin - Manuel Miranda earned a lot of praise, including best actor in a leading role in a musical. The production eventually won 11 Tonys, falling one short of the record. Hamilton was named best musical, and Lin - Manuel Miranda won for best book and original score. In July that year he made his final appearance in the show. In the following year, 2017, the musical opened in London's West End, where it was a critical and commercial success. It won seven Olivier Awards, including best new play. In addition, Lin - Manuel Miranda garnered the award for outstanding achievement in music. Hamilton has since been performed in a number of other cities outside the United States and across the country. A filmed performance of the musical aired on Disney+ in 2020.

Dance

The Musical is a vibrant and integral part of the show’s storytelling, combining various styles like hip-hop, jazz, and contemporary movement. Choreographed by Andy Blankenbuehler, the dance numbers reflect the high energy and intensity of the narrative, seamlessly weaving through the songs to amplify emotions and plot points. The ensemble's precise, synchronised movements serve as both a visual backdrop and a storytelling device, portraying everything from the chaos of battle to the inner turmoil of the characters.

Through its dynamic choreography, Hamilton captures the urgency of revolution and personal ambition, creating a visual spectacle that matches the rhythmic pulse of the music and lyrics.

Music

The Musical is a ground-breaking blend of genres, fusing hip-hop, R&B, pop, and traditional Broadway show tunes. The score reflects the diverse backgrounds and energetic spirit of America's Founding Fathers, reimagining their historical story in a modern context. Hip-hop serves as a key vehicle for expressing the revolutionary ideas and rapid-fire exchanges of characters like Alexander Hamilton and Aaron Burr, while more soulful ballads, like "Burn" and "Wait for It," offer emotional depth and reflection. The eclectic musical styles not only make the show fresh and innovative, but also help to bridge the gap between history and contemporary culture, engaging a wide audience with its dynamic storytelling.

There are 47 songs in the Broadway musical Hamilton. The number 47 is significant because it is the age at which Alexander Hamilton died. The musical runs for 2 hours and 45 minutes, including an intermission.
