All Things Hamilton

Lin - Manuel Miranda

Lin-Manuel Miranda was born January 16, 1980 in New York. He is an American actor, composer lyricist, and writer who created and starred in stage productions that blend modern musical forms, such as hip-hop, with classical musical theatre. His best-known work being Hamilton.

He was born to parents of Puerto Rican origin and grew up in a Hispanic neighbourhood in northern Manhattan. His father was a political consultant to several New York City mayors, and his mother was a psychologist.

In high school he won the lead tole in W.S. Gilbert and Arthur Sullivan's Pirates of Penzance and became a fixture in the school's drama program. He studied theatre at Wesleyan University, where he continued to perform in musicals and to write his own songs and shows. It was there that he created the first draft of the musical In the Heights, which was set in Washington Heights, a northern Manhattan neighbourhood similar to that of his childhood.

After reading Ron Chernow's 2004 biography about Alexander Hamilton, he began creating a musical about the Founding Father. The resulting Hamilton was energetic and infectious, and it features a racially diverse set, with Lin-Manuel Miranda starring the title role.

Later on, he contributed to the soundtrack for the Disney animated film Moana (2016), and he earned an Academy Award nomination - his first - for the song "How Far I'll Go". Also, in 2021, he wrote "Dos Orguitas" for the film Encanto, and the song earned him another Oscar nomination.

Background of Alexander Hamilton

Read time: 5 minutes

Based on the life of Alexander Hamilton, considered to be one of America's Founding Father's. Alexander Hamilton's face is imprinted on the $10 bill in the US. He was born in the Caribbean and moved to New York as a teenager to get an education. He had a strong interest in politics, he went on to become a soldier, statesman and scholar who founded the US legal system. Hamilton led three battalions in successful assaults on Yorktown, leading to the British choosing to surrender and ending the war. After the war, Hamilton went onto a political career, and in 1789 he was appointed by George Washington as the first US Secretary of the Treasury.

Hamilton the Musical

Read time: 5 minutes

Hamilton has a book, music and lyrics by Lin - Manuel Miranda, is directed by Thomas Kail, with choreography by Andy Blankenbuehler and musical supervision and orchestrations by Alex Lacamoire and is based on Ron Chernow's biography of Alexander Hamilton.

Hamilton, stage musical by American composer and lyricist Lin-Manuel Miranda that premiered Off-Broadway on February 17, 2015, at the Public Theatre in New York City before moving to Broadway for a second opening on August 6. The musical has been staged at the Victoria Palace Theatre in London since December 2017.

The explosively popular, composed over a seven-year period form 2008 - 2015, critically acclaimed musical combines hip-hop and Broadway in never seen way before. Lin-Manuel Miranda's work lifted Hamilton higher in the pantheon of the Founding Fathers, while humanising him in touching and inspiring ways. Many people consider this musical to be the most significant new American musical in this generation.

In 2016 Hamilton was awarded the Pulitzer Prize for Drama, and it received an unprecedented 16 Tony nominations. Lin - Manuel Miranda earning several nods, including best actor in a leading role in a musical. The production eventually won 11 Tonys, falling one short of the record. Hamilton was named best musical, and Lin - Manuel Miranda won for best book and original score. In July that year he made his final appearance in the show. In the following year, the musical opened in London's West End, where it was a critical and commercial success. It won seven Olivier Awards, including best new play. In addition, Lin - Manuel Miranda garnered the award for outstanding achievement in music. Hamilton has since been performed in a number of other cities outside the United States and across the country. A filmed performance of the musical aired on Disney+ in 2020.

The story of Alexander Hamilton speaks to audiences on so many levels. The motifs of revolution and fighting for one's rights, the power of people coming together to fight for a common cause, a celebration of freedom and democracy, and its messages of hope and perseverance resonate deeply with audiences. The resounding success of Hamilton is undeniable. Not only has it become one of the most popular musical of our time, but the show is also building future generations of theatre lovers, drawn to the music. The musical is a testament to the power of art and the importance of representation in theatre, Hamilton's fantastic score and deeper themes have made the story of one of America's Founding Fathers relevant worldwide.
